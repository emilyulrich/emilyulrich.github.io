About Me

Hello, my name is Emily Ulrich. I stumbled upon the joy of making maps not long after I graduated from my Master’s program and I’ve been grateful ever since. My career began in environmental science where I had many adventures from studying thermokarst lakes in Siberia to living on a wildlife refuge. As my passion for science grew, so did my desire to communicate what I had learned with the public. Maps became the perfect tool for communication. Once I began making maps for work, I was hooked and turned my focus to GIS.

Although I haven’t been in this field long, I have a large appetite for learning and problem solving. I have been fortunate to have wonderful mentors along the way and I’m looking forward to seeing where this passion can take me.

I grew up in Nevada where I enjoyed exploring the Sierra Nevada Mountains through my family’s annual horse pack trip. I love horse-back riding, hiking, tennis and traveling.
